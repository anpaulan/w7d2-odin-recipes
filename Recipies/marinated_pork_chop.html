Marinaded Pork Chops

Description

Quick and easy marinade for a one pan pork chop dish. Simple 5 ingredient recipe for a weeknight dinner. Quick and minimal preperation using pork chops, soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, onion(optional), and garlic(optional). All you really need is 30 minutes to marinade, but the longer the better.

Ingredients

  • Pork chops
  • Quarter cup of soy sauce (per pork chop)
  • Quarter cup of balsamic vinegar (per pork chop)
  • Half an Onion per chop (optional, but recommended)
  • Garlic, as little or as much as you want (optional, but recommended)

Steps

  1. Take equal parts balsamic and soy and add it to a container or a ziplock
  2. Try to cover most of the pork chops, but optionally you can flip the chops over to get the most out of your marinade if you are not using a ziplock bag
  3. Slice your onions into match sticks
  4. Mince your garlic
  5. Add the onions and garlic to your marinade
  6. Add a high smoke point oil to the pan on medium to high heat
  7. Cook the pork chop until your desired doneness, flip occasionally to prevent burning
  8. Add water to the pan if you feel your glaze is reducing too quickly to prevent burning
  9. Let your pork chops rest: ideally equal to the amount of time spent cooking
  10. If you added onions, add them to the pan on medium to medium high heat
  11. Cook the onions until your desired texture is achieved
  12. If you want to take your onions further, add water and lower the heat to get more carmelized onions
  13. The marinade should have thickened up to a glaze texture
